WeRateDogs Twitter Archive Report

Data Worked With:

The WeRateDogs twitter account is a series of tweets about different submitted dogs that have a rating and short blurb about them. They are intentionally humorous and with little meaning behind either the ratings or the descriptors.

I retrieved the data of almost 2,400 tweets out of over 5,000 between November 15, 2015 and July 1st, 2017 by accessing Twitter's API. I also programmatically downloaded the retweet and favorite counts (not included in the original data provided) using the tweet IDs.

Lastly I received an image predictions file from Udacity which gives the top three dog breed predictions per image.

Data Wrangling:

Once all the data had been retrieved there was the matter of bringing it together.

My main goals for the wrangling and cleaning were:

  • making the data presentable and clear for others who might be viewing it.
  • making the data clean enough to create accurate and understandable visualizations
  • removing misleading or irrelevant data that would make the first two goals more difficult\

Once I had cleaned and organized the data as I saw fit, there were a few insights I was able to draw (detailed below).

Insights:

Golden Retriever was by far the most tweeted about breed

This isn't a huge surprise, since it's such a well known and beloved breed. However this insight lead into a more interesting one:

Pembroke Appears Most Often in the Top 15 Highest Rated Dogs

Even though it only ranked third in the most tweeted about, more Pembrokes received a higher rating than any other breed of dog. Appearing three times in the top 15 highest rated dogs. This data makes a lot of sense when you see how absolutely adorable this breed is.

There’s a Clear Turning Point in the Popularity of the WeRateDogs Twitter:

As time went on, an interesting trend appears in the popularity of WeRateDogs. The twitter account started with a strong, if modest, bump in favorites. Over the next several months it seems like there were diminished returns and a decrease in popularity of tweets until about June of 2016 when some very popular posts emerged, and lead to a steady, but undeniable increase in favorites over time. It warrants investigating further what changed in the handling of the twitter to cause this impressive comeback (if it was a change in format, content, number of posts, etc…)

There are Far more Favorites than Retweets:

There are about double as many favorites as retweets, but that’s not too shocking as I feel people are much more likely to favorite a tweet than to share it on their own feed, especially when referring to tweets about dogs that are not their own. This also leads to another interesting insight:

There is a Very Clear Positive Correlation Between Favorites and Retweets:

Again not too surprising, but still interesting to note that it’s very clear that the higher the number of times a tweet was favorited, the higher the chance that it was also retweeted a larger number of times.
